# EKHUFT MEMORI Staff FAQ (Draft)

## 1) What is MEMORI?
MEMORI is an AI-enabled clinical decision support tool embedded in Sunrise. It helps clinicians identify **emerging infection risk** by analysing routine clinical data already in the record.

## 2) Is MEMORI replacing NEWS2?
No. MEMORI does **not** replace NEWS2. The two tools support different aspects of care and should be used together with clinical judgement and Trust escalation pathways.

## 3) What is the main difference between NEWS2 and MEMORI?
- **NEWS2:** detects physiological deterioration.
- **MEMORI:** highlights infection risk earlier.

## 4) What data does each tool use?
- **NEWS2** uses vital signs such as respiratory rate, heart rate, blood pressure, oxygen saturation, temperature, and level of consciousness.
- **MEMORI** uses multiple data sources including observations, blood test results, medications, and clinical trends over time.

## 5) What is each tool designed to detect?
- **NEWS2** is designed to detect **acute physiological deterioration**.
- **MEMORI** is designed to detect **patterns associated with infection risk**.

## 6) Why might MEMORI flag risk before NEWS2 increases?
MEMORI may identify infection-risk patterns **before** deterioration becomes physiologically visible in routine vital signs.

## 7) Can NEWS2 and MEMORI scores differ?
Yes. A high NEWS2 score can occur for reasons not related to infection, and a higher NEWS2 score does not necessarily mean a higher MEMORI risk level.

## 8) What should I do if NEWS2 and MEMORI do not align?
Use clinical judgement. Reassess the patient, review the broader clinical context, and discuss concerns with the nurse in charge and/or a senior clinician according to local escalation processes.

## 9) Does MEMORI diagnose infection?
No. MEMORI is an advisory tool to support earlier recognition and prioritisation. It does not diagnose infection and does not replace clinical assessment.

## 10) What should happen after a higher MEMORI risk level is seen?
Treat it as an additional clinical signal: perform focused review, consider infection in differential assessment, and escalate according to ward and Trust policy.

## 11) Where can I see MEMORI in Sunrise?
MEMORI appears in Sunrise views already used in routine workflow (e.g., Patient List, Tracker Board, and Flowsheet, depending on local configuration).

## 12) Who do I contact for support?
Speak to your ward lead/MEMORI champion or contact the local implementation support route in your Trust communications.
