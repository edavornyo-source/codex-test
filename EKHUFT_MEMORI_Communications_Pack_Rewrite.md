This pack is designed to build awareness, understanding, and adoption of MEMORI among clinical teams on Harvey Ward ahead of the planned go-live in **April 2026**.

## 4) EKHUFT Trust Bulletin
**Caption:** *Trust Bulletin | MEMORI Pilot | Harvey Ward*

### MEMORI Coming to Harvey Ward
**AI clinical decision support for earlier infection-risk recognition**

### What is MEMORI?
MEMORI is an AI-enabled clinical decision support tool embedded within Sunrise. It reviews routine clinical information already recorded in the patient record — including observations, blood results, and medications — to generate an infection-risk level:

**Low | Moderate | High | Critical**

MEMORI helps clinical teams identify emerging infection risk earlier. It supports clinical assessment but does not replace clinical judgement, NEWS2, or Trust escalation pathways.

### Why EKHUFT is introducing MEMORI
Hospital-acquired infections can begin developing before they are clinically obvious. MEMORI provides an additional signal to help clinicians recognise infection-related deterioration earlier and prioritise patient review.

The Harvey Ward pilot is part of a collaboration between EKHUFT and Sanome, a UK health technology company developing AI-enabled clinical decision support tools. The aim is to explore how AI-enabled tools can support:
- Earlier recognition of infection risk
- Faster clinical review and intervention
- Improved patient safety and outcomes
- Reduced cognitive load for clinical teams

MEMORI appears directly within Sunrise in views teams already use, including the Patient List, Tracker Board, and Flowsheet.

The MEMORI icon may appear within Sunrise; however, MEMORI is currently available only for patients on Harvey Ward.

### Co-design with clinical teams
A key feature of MEMORI’s development is its human-centred co-design approach. Rather than introducing technology from the top down, MEMORI has been shaped **with clinicians, not for them**.

Nurses, HCAs, consultants, and therapists have helped refine alerts, align workflows, and ensure the system supports real clinical practice without adding burden. This collaborative approach helps ensure the technology fits everyday care and supports safe clinical decision-making.

Experience from previous deployments shows that when clinicians help shape digital tools, adoption is easier and technology becomes a natural part of clinical workflow.

### What this means for Harvey Ward
MEMORI will sit alongside existing monitoring and escalation processes on the ward. It will be used together with NEWS2 and current Trust escalation pathways, providing an additional signal to support earlier clinical awareness.

Training sessions, quick reference guides, and ward champions will support staff during the early phase of the pilot.
